The Tenth Ritual: Thirst Eternal


In the black-glass archives of the Tower of Eternal Dominion, where living books whispered secrets older than the Fifteen Planes themselves, the Tenth Ritual was never written down in full. It existed only as a half-remembered tremor in the silver lattice collar, a forbidden cadence that even Lirael, the crimson-eyed albino sorceress, spoke of in lowered tones. The Vaadagh called it *Thirst Eternal*. The Denryssii named it simply *the Binding That Devours Both*.


It was born in the first trembling instant of the Second Conjunction, when Azarok’s laughter cracked the void and the moons first tasted each other’s blood. The Duke of Hell did not create the ritual; he merely remembered it. It was the same ache that had once made the first star realise it would die - that exquisite, unbearable need to drink from another until the line between self and other dissolved into starlight and ruin.


When the iron collar is locked around a captive’s throat and the tenth cadence is whispered, the ritual does not merely sip life-force in delicate, ecstatic pulses. It *merges*. Captor and captive become a single closed circuit of hunger. Every heartbeat, every breath, every tremor of pleasure or pain is echoed and amplified between them. The Vaadagh scholar feels the crude, fertile heat of the Mabden warlord flooding her veins; the warlord tastes the cold, luminous despair of dying empires blooming inside his scarred chest. Contempt and obsessive desire cease to be opposites. They become the same molten current.


The Tenth Ritual does not kill. It *shares*. It feeds the stolen essence back and forth in an endless loop, so that the captive’s luminous beauty strengthens the captor’s brutality, and the captor’s raw vitality keeps the captive’s fragile starlight from guttering out. Yet the price is merciless: neither can ever again be whole without the other. Should one be slain, the other will bleed starlight and iron in equal measure until the planes themselves forget their names.


In the moment of climax the ritual reaches its cruel perfection. Pleasure becomes philosophical despair made flesh. The captive arches and sobs, feeling not only her own surrender but the captor’s ancient, half-forgotten longing for something beautiful enough to ruin him. The captor groans against her throat, tasting not only conquest but the cold grandeur of a dying empire that has chosen, for one trembling heartbeat, to open its legs and its soul to the very herd it once scorned.


Azarok laughs loudest during the Tenth Ritual, because he knows the final truth: when the Conjunction reaches its peak and the Fifteen Planes collapse into one another like lovers in their death-throes, the only beings who will still feel anything at all will be those bound by Thirst Eternal. They will burn together, come together, and perhaps - in the velvet darkness between one world’s end and the next’s beginning - learn to love the very destruction that joined them.


That is the Tenth Ritual.


Not a spell.


Not a torment.


A cosmic marriage written in blood, starlight, and the slow, eternal thirst of a god who has always known that the most exquisite surrender is the one that can never be undone.

Azarok does not rule the Fifteen Planes. He seduces them.


He is not the architect of the Conjunction; he is its lover, its pimp, its laughing witness. Where the Vaadagh built their crystal mansions in cold, perfect isolation and the Mabden forged their bronze-and-iron lamellar in the hot blood of conquest, Azarok simply waited in the velvet dark between heartbeats. He is the moment the first star understood it would die, and he has spent eternity ensuring every living thing tastes that same exquisite, unbearable knowledge.


In the story of Elyndra and Kael, Azarok plays every part at once.


He is the iron collar locked around her throat that drinks in slow, ecstatic pulses.

He is the savage heat of Kael’s scarred body pressing her against ancient Iranian pillars while the double moons bleed overhead.

He is the Tenth Ritual itself - Thirst Eternal - the closed circuit that now binds captor and captive so tightly that neither can draw breath without tasting the other’s soul.


Azarok does not want the Vaadagh exterminated. He wants them *claimed*. He does not want the Denryssii victorious. He wants them *changed* - forever marked by the luminous, decadent flesh they once called “parasites.” Every time Kael sinks into Elyndra and feels her ancient despair bloom inside his own chest, Azarok laughs low and intimate, because the crude herd-beast is learning to love the very beauty he was sent to destroy. Every time Elyndra arches and sobs his name while the collar feeds Kael’s raw vitality back into her veins, Azarok leans closer, delighted that the proud scholar of dying empires has begun to crave the very savagery she once scorned.


He is the shadow behind Glandytha-Cree’s hunters, the crimson glint on Vargul’s spear, the reason the warbands ride not merely to burn but to *possess*. He is the velvet voice that whispers to the exiled Elmiboran lord and his crimson-eyed consort inside the black-glass Tower of Eternal Dominion: *Break them slowly. Make them love the breaking.*


Above all, Azarok is the cosmic matchmaker of ruin.


He has arranged this particular captivity with the care of a connoisseur. The proud Vaadagh astronomer taken alive from her burning mansion by the scarred Denryssii champion is no accident. The slow-burn power exchange between cultural contempt and obsessive desire is his masterpiece. Every philosophical confession gasped between kisses, every moment of unexpected tenderness (Kael shielding her naked body with his own, Elyndra teaching her captor the beauty of a dying star), every ritualistic pulse of the collar - all of it is Azarok conducting the final movement of the Conjunction.


When the Fifteen Planes finally collapse into one another like lovers in their death-throes, Azarok will not stand triumphant above the wreckage. He will simply lean back in the dark, smiling, and watch the last two beings still capable of feeling anything - a silver-haired scholar and a bronze-ringed warlord - burn together in the exquisite, eternal circuit of Thirst Eternal.


Because Azarok has always known the final truth of every empire, every scholar, every savage:


Nothing is more erotic than the moment just before annihilation.

The Conjunction was coming, and he meant to greet it with the last beautiful thing in the cosmos kneeling at his feet.
